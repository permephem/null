# Null Protocol Economic Framework

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** September 2025  
**Status:** ICO-Ready Economic Model

---

## Executive Summary

The Null Protocol operates on a **dual-layer economic model** that combines enterprise fiat payments with NULL token-based protocol settlement. This design ensures accessibility for traditional businesses while creating sustainable token demand and value capture.

**Key Innovation:** The "Obol" fee structure (12/13 to implementers, 1/13 to Foundation) creates aligned incentives between commercial operators and protocol development.

---

## 1. Token Economics Overview

### 1.1 NULL Token Fundamentals

- **Network:** Ethereum (ERC-20)
- **Total Supply:** 1,000,000,000 NULL (fixed, no inflation)
- **Utility:** Protocol settlement currency for all on-chain operations
- **Governance:** Foundation treasury management and protocol upgrades

### 1.2 Dual-Layer Payment System

#### Layer 1: Enterprise Interface (Fiat)

- Enterprises pay deletion fees in traditional currencies (USD, EUR, etc.)
- Null Engine converts fiat to NULL tokens for protocol settlement
- Removes cryptocurrency friction for traditional businesses
- Enables predictable pricing and accounting

#### Layer 2: Protocol Settlement (NULL)

- All on-chain operations require NULL tokens
- Smart contract fees, relayer payments, treasury funding
- Creates consistent token demand regardless of fiat volatility
- Ensures protocol sustainability through token economics

## 2. The Obol Fee Structure

Named after the coin placed on the eyes of the dead in Greek mythology, the Obol represents payment for digital death services.

### 2.1 Fee Distribution

For every deletion processed:

- **12/13 (92.3%)** → Commercial implementer (Null Engine)
- **1/13 (7.7%)** → Null Foundation treasury

### 2.2 Economic Rationale

#### Implementer Incentives (12/13)

- Sustainable business model for service providers
- Covers operational costs: infrastructure, compliance, monitoring
- Enables competitive pricing while maintaining quality
- Encourages ecosystem growth through profitability

#### Foundation Funding (1/13)

- Protocol development and maintenance
- Security audits and upgrades
- Community programs and grants
- Governance infrastructure
- Regulatory compliance and advocacy

## 4. Value Capture Mechanisms

### 4.1 Token Velocity Management

#### Staking Requirements

- Implementers must stake NULL tokens for service authorization
- Longer staking periods reduce circulating supply
- Slashing conditions for non-compliance

#### Treasury Reserves

- Foundation maintains 30% of total supply in reserves
- Strategic releases based on ecosystem needs
- Long-term price stability through managed supply

### 4.2 Deflationary Pressures

#### Fee Burning (Future Implementation)

- Percentage of fees permanently removed from circulation
- Reduces total supply over time
- Increases scarcity as adoption grows

#### Lost Token Recovery

- Unclaimed tokens from failed transactions
- Expired warrant tokens returned to treasury
- Natural supply reduction through operational losses
